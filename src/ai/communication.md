# Communication Guide

## Pattern: Pitching a System Migration

1. **Lead with their benefit**, not your work
2. **Ground urgency in real events** they already felt
3. **Be honest about gaps** — it builds trust and invites help
4. **Frame the ask as collaboration**, not a demand
5. **State the end goal clearly** so people can orient
6. **Put their priorities first** explicitly
7. **Make the next step easy and concrete**

### Why Each Works

- First sentence frames the change in terms of benefits to the recipient — not "we built a thing"
- Reference a concrete, recent pain point the audience experienced — makes the ask feel like it's in their interest
- Admit gaps honestly ("almost reaches parity") — builds trust without alarming
- Position the audience as experts whose knowledge is needed — people help when they feel valued, not directed
- No ambiguity about what success looks like — "turn off X and just use Y"
- "Anything you need will be our top priority" — flips the power dynamic, removes friction
- Suggest a specific time and concrete goal — not "let's sync sometime"
